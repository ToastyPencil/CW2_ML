\begin{abstract}
This report reproduces the low-budget TypiClust setting of Hacohen et al.~\cite{hacohen2022active} on CIFAR-10 using self-supervised image representations, then evaluates an adaptive modification that preserves pure typicality in the cold-start regime and gradually adds novelty and uncertainty later. The final pipeline used 500 SimCLR pretraining epochs on an RTX 3070 and compared Random, Entropy, TypiClust, and TypiClust+Adaptive over five active learning rounds with matched seeds.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
Active learning reduces annotation cost by querying only a small subset of examples for labeling. Hacohen et al.~\cite{hacohen2022active} argue that the low-budget regime is special: when only a few labels are available per round, representative examples can matter more than classical uncertainty heuristics. CIFAR-10 is a useful benchmark for testing that claim because it is widely used, computationally manageable, and still exposes whether a query strategy can cover the data distribution under severe label scarcity.

\section{Methodology}
TypiClust computes an embedding of the unlabeled pool, clusters the pool, and repeatedly selects the most typical point from the cluster with the fewest labeled examples. Typicality is measured as inverse average distance to nearest neighbours:
\[
\text{Typicality}(x) = \left(\frac{1}{K} \sum_{x_i \in K\text{-NN}(x)} \lVert x-x_i \rVert_2\right)^{-1}.
\]
Diversity is therefore enforced through clustering, while representativeness is enforced within each cluster through local density. The reproduction uses CIFAR-10, a SimCLR-style ResNet-18 encoder~\cite{chen2020simclr} for self-supervised representation learning, L2-normalized embeddings, and a supervised ResNet-18 classifier retrained after each query round. Following Appendix F of the paper, clustering uses \(K=\min(|L|+B,\texttt{max\_clusters})\), typicality uses up to 20 neighbours, and tiny clusters are filtered before selection.

\section{Algorithm Modification}
The targeted improvement pass replaces the original novelty-only variant with a late-stage hybrid score. The modified method keeps pure TypiClust in the earliest low-budget rounds, activates a normalized novelty term once the labeled set has initial coverage, and then adds uncertainty only in later rounds when the classifier has enough supervision to make entropy informative:
\[
\text{Score}(x)=\widehat{\text{Typicality}}(x)+\lambda_t \cdot \widehat{\text{Novelty}}(x)+\mu_t \cdot \widehat{\text{Entropy}}(x),
\]
where \(\lambda_t\) increases from round 3, \(\mu_t\) increases from round 4, and all terms are min-max normalized within the chosen cluster. This is justified by the paper's budget-dependent argument and by the tuning evidence from the reproduction: when the labeled pool is almost empty, representative points should dominate; after that, novelty improves coverage; only later should the method trust classifier uncertainty. The evaluation therefore compares TypiClust and TypiClust+Adaptive with identical seeds, budgets, cached SSL embeddings, and training settings.
